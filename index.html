Assignment 1

 

After extensive internet research, we came into contact with Erik Spronk. 

We asked him for the necessary information about the “the surface water quality that is monitored by Waternet”, so he referred us to his colleague, Joost Stoffels.

 

We emailed him asking for data about the water quality in Amsterdam canals, and he answered with 2 web links: national database of surface water quality data and an interesting map about water quality.

 

From the first link we could find 2 different CSV files, we used the IM-measurements-2022 for this assignment. The first one gave us the locations of the measurements, while the second one gave us the values of different properties like temperature, concentrations of chemical elements and pH.

 

They are both readable by humans but at the same time there is a lot of data. A Python script is needed,

The data is numerical, but it also has descriptions, so we would say it is alphanumeric.

 

There is also geo-spatial data in the file with the locations. It has the X and Y coordinates of every measurement point.

 

The data is temporal. Every measurement point has a specific time and date. The interval is 2 months, which means that we can have a CSV file for every two months of the selected year.
